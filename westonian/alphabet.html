the westonian language has 25 letters and 5 digraphs, all of which have been listed here with a sample pronounciation. there are only 2 letters in westonian that can be made long, and the other vowels always make their default sound. the letters A and I can be modified into long vowels by placing an apostrophe (') before the letter in a word (example: phangente = fawn-genteh; pha'ngente = fain-genteh). the word's definition DOES depend on whether or not any of the vowels are long (so phangente and pha'ngente COULD mean different things).
in westonian, pronounciation is always constant. all letters found in westonian will always be pronounced the ways they are here. it can be difficult to remember this at times, especially if you speak a language which has pronounciations which depend on context, but pronouncing each letter correctly is key to speaking clearly, as westonian words can often sound very similar to one another.
in westonian, there are no names for the letter other than their default and unmodified pronounciation. for instance, B is called "buh," and D is called "duh."
