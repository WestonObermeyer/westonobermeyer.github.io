pronounciation of letters in words (as if being read in American English)

A = Ah (variations: aa = long a (ay))

C = Ss (pronounced as an "S" would be in American English)

E = Eh (letter E followed by any other vowel makes a "yuh" sound before that vowel, then the following vowel is made long. for example: eo (yo), ei (yiye), eu (you).)

(same as in American English - however, there is no variation. "G" in Westonian is NEVER pronounced "j," or any other way besides the usual "guh.")
